Ludo

Ali Dorri

Ludo is a multiplayer board game consists of 4 players.

History

Pachisi was created in India in the 6th century. The earliest evidence of this game's evolution in India is the depiction of boards on the caves of Ellora.

Pachisi was modified to use a cubic die with dice cup and patented as "Ludo" in England in 1896. The Royal Navy took Ludo and converted it into the board game Uckers.

Rules

Gameplay

Player play the game in a turn based mode. Each player it his/her turn roll the dice and do action according to the given number.
If it's 1-5, player can move any of his/her marbles in the game equal to that number.
If it's 6 player can move a marble into the game or move another marble. Also 6 number gives the player permission to roll the dice again.

Player marbles cannot move the slot which is occupied by one of his/her other marbles. If player move a marble to a slot of an opponent's marble, it hits that marble and makes it go out of the game.

Win & Lose

The goal of game is to move all of your marbles to the end slots. The winner is the first one who does so.
